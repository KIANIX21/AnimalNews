New Study Reveals How 469 Bird Species Are Extinct Due to Humans!

New findings released by Tel Aviv University (TAU) and the Weizmann Institute of Science reveal how Humanity has made 469 bird species on earth extinct. this latest study shows that hundreds of bird species are extinct with humans as the main culprit, said TAU in their press release.

Researchers say some significant characteristics make the birds easily available to humans and para animal hunters who catch them to eat them. This resulted in the extinction of 469 species of these birds.

In the last 20,000 to 50,000 years, about 10 to 20 percent of all bird species have become extinct, according to the study. This study report has been published in the Journal of Biogeography on 3 July 2021.

The study was led by Professor Shai Meiri from the School of Zoology at George S. Wise Faculty of Life Sciences dan Steinhardt Museum of Natural History at TAU, and Amir Fromm from the Weizmann Institute. In this study they noted that most of the extinct bird species bear some resemblance.

The bird species are large, inhabit the islands, and most of them cannot fly. Researchers say these characteristics make the birds readily available to humans and other animal hunters who looking for them for food.

In this study, the researchers also showed hope that their discovery will help avoid further bird extinction.

"Our study shows that prior to major extinction events in the the past thousands of years, more large birds, even giants, as well can not fly live on our earth, and the diversity of birds that live on the islands were much bigger than today," said Shai Meiri speerti reported by Nature World News.

Meiri said she and Amir Fromm hoped their discovery could play a role role as a warning signal regarding current bird species endangered. He also added that it was important to know whether The birds that are being threatened with extinction have unique characteristics same.

However, it must be acknowledged that the conditions that threaten the extinction of the birds today has changed greatly. Now, the main cause of extinction bird species by humans are not the result of hunting, but rather the destruction of the birds' natural habitat.

For example, Israel noticed a great flood of birds who migrate to the country every year. A large number of birds return to the shores of Eilat following the migration pattern in mid-April.

However, over the last eight years, the number of birds that came to Eilath, the southernmost tip of Israel, has decreased sharply. This is in accordance with data collected by the Center Eilat International Birding and Research Research Center Eilat), which further made experts worry that the rate of extinction of the birds is increasing.

In early August 2021, Israel's Minister of Environmental Protection, Tamar Zandberg, made an announcement that the country would expand wildlife protection of two bird species.

Israeli government plans to ban hunting of turtle pigeons for three years. They also banned hunting for quail completely. Experts hope this policy can prevent further declines occurred in the populations of the two bird species.
